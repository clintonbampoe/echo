# Summary

---

## Quick Overview

- **Membership Management** is the foundation: everything else ties back to members and groups.

- **Core Accounting** is the financial backbone: all inflows/outflows pass through it.

- **Reporting** is the integrator: it pulls from every other module.

- **Attendance** and **Projects** enrich the picture with engagement and initiatives.

- **Assets** ensure long‑term accountability.

---

## Functional Modules

### 1. Membership Management

- Maintains records of individual members and groups.

- Links members to contributions, attendance, and roles.

- Provides accountability at both individual and group levels.

### 2. Core Accounting

- Records all income and expenditure.

- Tracks balances across accounts (cash, bank, designated funds).

- Provides the financial backbone for the system.

### 3. Contributions

- Tracks member contributions, tithes, and organizational dues.

- Supports accountability by linking contributions to members and groups.

- Feeds directly into Core Accounting and Reporting.

### 4. Projects

- Manages project funds, payments, and fundraising.

- Tracks progress against budgets and completion stages.

- Provides visibility into initiatives beyond routine operations.

### 5. Assets

- Maintains records of physical and financial assets.

- Tracks acquisition, valuation, and designated funds.

- Ensures long‑term accountability for resources.

### 6. Attendance

- Records participation in services and events.

- Links attendance to members and groups.

- Provides engagement statistics for analysis.

### 7. Reporting

- Generates financial statements and analytical reports.

- Integrates data from all modules for transparency and decision‑making.

- Supports external reporting requirements (circuit, diocese, auditors).
